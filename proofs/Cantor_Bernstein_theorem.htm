Theorem Cantor_Bernstein_theorem

Theorem. Cantor_Bernstein_theorem
set X → num_le X Y → num_le Y X → num_eq X Y

This theorem states that not greater than behaves antisymmetric, that is, |X| = |Y| follows from |X| ≤ |Y| and |Y| ≤ |X|. In words, X and Y are equinumerous if, on the one hand, X is not greater than Y and, on the other hand, Y is not greater than X. This means that a bijection from X to Y exists if there is an injection from X to Y on the one hand and an injection from Y to X on the other.

Proof idea
According to the premises, there is an injection from X to Y and an injection from Y to X. Using the Banach decomposition theorem, X and Y can now be decomposed into disjoint subsets such that the injections take the form of bijections when restricted to these subsets. For the latter, we consider the inverse mapping so that both go in the same direction. These two bijections can now be joined together to form a bijection from X to Y.

Proof sketch
According to the premises, we have an injection f: X → Y and an injection g: Y → X. Furthermore, according to the Banach decomposition theorem, there exist A ⊆ X and B ⊆ Y such that f[A] = B and g[Y\B] = X\A. Therefore, the restrictions f|A: A → B and g|Y\B: Y\B → X\A are bijective. Consequently, the inverse of g|Y\B is also bijective. The two can now be combined via bij_union to form a bijection X → Y, since both X = A ∪ (X\A) and Y = B ∪ (Y\B) are unions of disjoint sets. q.e.d.
