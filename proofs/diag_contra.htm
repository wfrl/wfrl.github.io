Theorem diag_contra

Theorem. diag_contra
(A ↔ ¬A) → ⊥

Lemma of diagonal arguments.

Proof sketch
Let A ↔ ¬A hold. A contradiction must be derived. Under the additional assumption A, ¬A follows by means of the premise, but this contradicts the assumption. Therefore, the assumption must be false, meaning that ¬A holds. From this, A follows by means of the premise, but this again results in a contradiction, which, in contrast to the previous one, depends only on the premise alone. q.e.d.

Proof
01. 1 ⊢ A ↔ ¬A, hypo.
02. 2 ⊢ A, hypo.
03. 1, 2 ⊢ ¬A, lsubj_elim 1 2.
04. 1, 2 ⊢ ⊥, neg_elim 3 2.
05. 1 ⊢ ¬A, neg_intro 4.
06. 1 ⊢ A, rsubj_elim 1 5.
07. 1 ⊢ ⊥, neg_elim 5 6.
diag_contra. ⊢ (A ↔ ¬A) → ⊥, subj_intro 7.
