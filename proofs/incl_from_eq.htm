Theorem incl_from_eq

Theorem. incl_from_eq
A = B → A ⊆ B

This theorem states that inclusion follows from equality.

Proof idea
This is essentially just a convoluted form of incl_refl.

Proof sketch
According to incl_refl, A ⊆ A holds. Because A = B, we may now substitute B for the A on the right-hand side, which leads to A ⊆ B. q.e.d

Proof
01. 1 ⊢ A = B, hypo.
02. ⊢ A ⊆ A, incl_refl.
03. 1 ⊢ A ⊆ B, eq_subst 1 2, P X ↔ A ⊆ X.
incl_from_eq. ⊢ A = B → A ⊆ B, subj_intro 3.

Dependencies
The given proof depends on one axiom:
eq_subst.
